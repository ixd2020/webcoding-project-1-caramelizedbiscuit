All about Shirin Neshat

Shirin Neshat, a contemporary Iranian visual artist best known for her photography and films. She has broken societal norms with her works that speaks volume. She challenges the ideology of Muslim women being victims and dives deep into exile through her works. Since Iran has undermined basic human rights, particularly since the Islamic Revolution, Shirin felt that she "gravitated toward making art that is concerned with tyranny, dictatorship, oppression and political injustice. Shirin once said, Although I don’t consider myself an activist, I believe my art – regardless of its nature – is an expression of protest, a cry for humanity.”

Shirin grew up in Qavin, north-western of Iran and is the fourth of five children of wealthy parents. She describes her home as a "very warm, supportive Muslim family environment". She grew up learning traditional religious values through her maternal grandparents. Furthermore, when she got older, she was enrolled in a Catholic boarding school in Tehran. She was surrounded and influenced by western feminism, as her father encouraged each of his daughters to "be an individual, to take risks, to learn, to see the world". Shirin's father sent his daughters as well as his sons to college to receive higher education

In 1975, Shirin left her hometown to study art at UC Berkeley and completed her BA, MA and MFA. She soon then graduated from UC Berkeley in 1983, and moved to New York City. However, while she was there she was quick to realise that making art wasn't going to be her full time profession at that point of time. Additionally, after meeting her future husband, who ran the Storefront for Art and Architecture, an alternative space in Manhattan, she decidedto dedicate ten years of her life to working with him there, as a second education. Her works are included in the collections of the Tate Gallery in London, The Museum of Modern Art in New York, the Tel Aviv Museum of Art, and the Walker Art Center in Minneapolis.
