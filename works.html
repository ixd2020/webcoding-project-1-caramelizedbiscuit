The Works of Shirin Neshat

Audio Installations Turbulent (1998), Rapture (1999), Soliloquy (1999), Fervor (2000), Passage (2001), Mahdokht (2004), Zarin (2005), Munis (2008), Faezeh (2008), Possession (2009)
Films Women Without Men (2009), Illusions & Mirrors (2013), Looking for Oum Kulthum (2017)
Bibliography Women of Allah, Two Installations, Shirin Neshat: 2002-2005, I Know Something about Love, Shirin Neshat: Toba


Shirin started off with photography and her earliest works include the Unveiling (1993) and Women of Allah (1993–97) series, which explore notions of femininity in relation to Islamic fundamentalism and militancy in her home country of Iran. As Shirin was experiencing discrepancy between the culture and that of the pre-revolution Iran in which she was raised, she began her first mature body of work, the Women of Allah series, portraits of women entirely overlaid by Persian calligraphy.Her work refers to the social, cultural and religious codes of Muslim societies and the complexity of certain oppositions, such as man and woman. Neshat often emphasizes this theme showing two or more coordinated films concurrently, creating stark visual contrasts through motifs such as light and dark, black and white, male and female. Neshat has also made more traditional narrative short films, such as Zarin (2005).
